BlockSync

Early Access Program
Seequent | Jul — Sep 2023

BlockSync is a revolutionary Horizon 3 software solution designed to streamline block model data management for geologists in the mining industry. It's aimed to provide geologists with version control, efficient data management, and comprehensive reporting capabilities for their block model data. The concept for BlockSync had been in development for some time, and in 2023 we finally launched an early access program to address the pressing challenges faced by geologists.

My contributions
• Personas • User research • Wire-framing • User testing • Survey design • Metrics • Roadmapping

BlockSync | Web view

Problem

Picture John, a resource geologist at a large mining company. It's crucial that the block models his team works with are up to date and accurate otherwise the company could face loss of investors and negative press if the reports he creates are inaccurate or faulty. His current solution is to store block models on shared network drives, resulting in a disorganized mess of folders with confusing names like "hagleyparkFinal" and "hagleyparkFinalJohn".
‍
BlockSync is a centralized repository of block models and version in a streamlined manner, while being able to be accessed through 3 methods: Integrations, APIs and the web interface. Additionally, it enables users to grant access permissions to their colleagues, fostering seamless collaboration and knowledge sharing.

Getting Involved

My involvement in the BlockSync project commenced a few months before the Early Access Program. At this point, the key priorities included:‍

  • ‍Review existing work: Conducting an in-depth examination of prior work and existing solutions to identify areas for improvement and potential gaps in the user experience. Analyzing the integration of BlockSync with Leapfrog, through usability testing sessions to ensure a seamless and intuitive experience.
  • Roadmapping: Crafting a UX roadmap to guide the development of BlockSync. Prioritizing key features such as Importing and Downloading block models, reporting, and block model management to streamline geological workflows and enhance user productivity.
  • Measuring success: To objectively evaluate the effectiveness of BlockSync's UX enhancements, we began to establish a set of measurable Key Performance Indicators (KPIs).

Usability Testing - Leapfrog Integration

The first program to be integrated with BlockSync is Leapfrog, the Seequent flagship product for 3D modelling. The goal of this testing was to assess the users' experience while performing essential workflow tasks. By conducting usability tests, we aimed to identify pain points, issues, and opportunities for enhancing the BlockSync platform. Additionally, we sought to determine whether BlockSync was ready for market release and whether it met an acceptable System Usability Score.

Goals
  • Assess the users’ experience of using BlockSync to achieve key workflow tasks
  • Identify problems and uncover opportunities for improvement of BlockSync
  • Assess whether BlockSync is ready for market, meets an acceptable usability score of 70 or above
Methodology
  • Moderated, remote, semi-structured interviews
  • 5 Leapfrog users
  • Microsoft Teams

Results
  • No visibility of system status: Users are not informed that the system is loading
  • Unclear terminology: 'Merge' Label does not match user’s mental model resulting in users taking incorrect paths, missing key steps and misunderstanding functionality. 4/5 users did not complete the task.

Task success matrix: Showing how users completed the tasks, and how they rated their experience.

Strategy & Advocacy

To effectively socialize the UX contributions across the company, we made a strategic decision to create artifacts such as a user testing plan, script, and results report. The report comprehensively outlined the identified problems and presented actionable recommendations for the product team.

Trade offs - Import & Export

To accommodate customers not using Leapfrog, we introduced the BlockSync web interface with the aim of ensuring a seamless workflow. Despite the inherent technical limitations in web development, such as multiple backend calls and unknown processing speeds for downloading, we prioritized user experience.

  • Importing block model in multiple steps: Choosing a multi-step approach for data safety. While a single-page input saves clicks, our method ensures data is saved incrementally, preventing users from starting over in case of errors.

Import block model screen, showing a wizard that saves their progress at each step.

  • Addressing unknown download speeds: Downloading a block model's duration varies based on its size, often taking several minutes. Due to resource limitations, a full-fledged download manager wasn't practical. Instead, we used effective UX writing in toast messages to keep users informed and engaged throughout the download journey.

Block model details screen, showing the toast notification message

Challenges

  • Establishing UX Metrics: Achieving alignment among Product, Development, and Customer Insights was challenging - from the technical feasibility of extracting pertinent data to thinking of the potential impact on existing surveys being sent out. Estimating the level of customers' survey responsiveness were also key aspects to consider as it would determine a need for incentives.
  • Prioritization: During PI planning, there were challenges with constant shifts in priorities, overlapping roadmaps, and a lack of standardized tools for seamless alignment. Embracing an experimental mindset, I successfully integrated the impact-effort matrix as a strategic tool to navigate and enhance the prioritization process.
Impact

Our efforts rewarded significant insights - The system usability score of 76 indicated a positive user perception, demonstrating the effectiveness of our decisions. Additionally, the Datadog dashboard provides valuable insights, enabling us to start setting key performance indicators and track longitudinal metrics to drive decisions.
