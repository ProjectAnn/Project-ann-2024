EVO

Workspaces
Seequent | May - November 2023

Evo is Seequent’s cloud-based ecosystem, a solution that enables customers and the industry at-large to collaborate and manage geoscience data in a single space through personalization and customization. It provides the foundation for connected workflows between Seequent and third-party applications.

Beginnings
Embedded design

Adopting the Discovery & Delivery model championed by the Silicon Valley Product Group, I integrated into a dynamic development team of five. I played a pivotal role by immersing myself, actively participating in scrum ceremonies and taking charge of the design discovery and delivery process. This enabled us to navigate the complexities of geoscience data management, consistently prioritizing user needs.

What's the problem?

Seequent customers grapple with large amounts of data every day. The challenge lies in the need for users to share intricate geological models and geoscience data across disciplines seamlessly. How do we enable customers to effortlessly share working data, finalized data and integrate multiple data sources? Who do we even pick as a key persona to start with? What about merging data? We started off with the delivery of Workspaces, a feature that would enable teams working on a geographical site to manage their data in a centralized manner.

Data management hierarchy

Take Sarah, a geologist doing analysis in Seequent software such as Driver and Leapfrog. She creates a trend* in Driver, then uploads it into Evo Workspaces to share with her team. She can also use that trend for herself to open in Leapfrog and make changes to her geological model.

Data Management Discovery

To begin discovery for Workspaces, our approach involved crafting initial sketches that emphasized the use of tags, folders, views, and projects. We investigated how customers manage their daily tasks, identifying their priorities and pinpointing activities perceived as unnecessary. This involved comprehending how proposed solutions would impact their existing workflows. Furthermore, we aimed to uncover the intricacies of participants' thought processes and mental models as they navigate and manage their data.

Results
  • No preference: Most didn't have a preference of folders or tags
  • Concerns on Scalability: Many had concerns on folders and their potential for becoming overloaded and disorganized
  • Projects: All were excited for projects, since the ability to merge between branches and initiate peer reviews was a feature they had repeatedly requested.
Learnings

‍From user feedback, we learned that users weren't too concerned about specific details, like whether they preferred tags or folders. The 'Projects' feature got a lot of positive reactions, so our next steps include planning detailed user tests for merging and peer reviewing. We're continuing our exploration in discovery sprints, refining our testing plans to focus on how users can easily share data, integrate different data sources, and efficiently publish 'objects' or 'objects and projects.'

Early ideation of 'Create promote request' feature, showing how users could request a peer review before merging.

Challenges

We navigated challenges that involved frequent pivoting, complexities in project management, project prioritization, synchronization with semiannual desktop product cycles, and determining the optimal level of granularity.

These challenges extended to adapting to a new payment model, addressing scalability concerns across two products, and maintaining a focused strategy tied to release times, such as BlockSync. Our solutions were guided not just by immediate concerns but also by a broader, long-term vision, involving critical trade-offs for success at both tactical and strategic levels.

Additionally, in the realm of user testing, we encountered diverse opinions from a limited pool of participants regarding what and how we should present to customers across different functions.

Impact

Evo has had a profound impact on Seequent's ecosystem, influencing numerous Seequent products and benefiting a wide range of customers by facilitating connected workflows. This transformative effect extends to how geoscience data is managed and shared. It also played a crucial role in our shift towards an empowered team model, representing a significant step forward. Our adoption of a cross-functional approach aligns with principles advocated by Marty Cagan, reinforcing our commitment to best practices in product development.
